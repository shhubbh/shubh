On the work

I work in the founder's office at Ottonomy, a company building L4 autonomous delivery robots. My job is to bridge the gap between what our robots can do and what our customers need them to do—which means closing enterprise deals, managing global deployments, and troubleshooting the thousand ways reality breaks our assumptions.

Before this, I led a 40-person student team building Martian rovers, worked on predictive analytics for mining drones, and studied mechanical engineering at BITS Pilani with a focus on robotics.

I'm most drawn to problems with physical constraints—systems that need to perform reliably in degraded environments, when no one's watching, when things go wrong.
